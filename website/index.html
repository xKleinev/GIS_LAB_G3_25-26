About the Project

Why air quality, why France, and what this website shows

Air pollution remains one of the largest environmental health risks in Europe. This project uses free and open geospatial data to reconstruct how nitrogen dioxide (NO2) and particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10) concentrations evolved across France between 2021 and 2023, and to relate those changes to land cover and population distribution.

  • Monthly and annual pollutant surfaces derived from the Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service (CAMS).
  • Annual Mean Aggregation Change (AMAC) mapped for each pollutant between 2021 and 2023.
  • Zonal statistics, bivariate pollution–population maps and an interactive WebGIS to explore every layer produced.
