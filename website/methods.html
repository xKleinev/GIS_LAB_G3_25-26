Processing Pipeline

Steps 1–8, following the Lab 2–4 requirements, applied to France

CAMS reanalysis rasters for NO2, PM2.5 and PM10 were clipped to France for December 2023 (FRANCE_CAMS_no2_2023_12.tif, etc.) and averaged across all months of 2023 to produce annual mean concentration surfaces (FRANCE_average_no2_2023.tif, etc.).

Describe your resampling / reprojection choices (target CRS, cell size) and any gap-filling you applied.

Annual averages were reclassified into WHO / EU air-quality concentration classes to produce FRANCE_no2_concentration_map_2023.tif and the PM2.5 / PM10 equivalents.

List the concentration class breakpoints you used and their source (e.g. WHO 2021 guidelines).

Raster difference between the 2023 and 2021 annual means for each pollutant, styled with a diverging blue→white→red ramp (FRANCE_no2_2021_2023_AMAC_map.tif + matching .qml style). These are the mandatory layers shown on the WebGIS.

Explain your AMAC formula (e.g. mean2023 − mean2021) and how you chose the class breaks in the QML style.

FRANCE_LCC_2021_2023.tif tracks land cover transitions between 2021 and 2023; the resulting transition matrix is published as the statistical table on the Results page.

Note which CORINE classes you grouped together and why, and highlight the most significant transitions you found.

Mean / min / max pollutant values were computed per region for each pollutant (FRANCE_no2_zonal_statistics_2021_2023.gpkg and equivalents) and charted on the Results page.

Mention the zonal statistics tool/algorithm used (e.g. QGIS "Zonal Statistics") and the region layer used as zones.

Each zone was classified simultaneously by pop_class_median and pol_class_max to build a 3×3 bivariate map per pollutant (FRANCE_no2_2023_bivariate.gpkg and equivalents), shown with its legend on the Results page.

Describe how the population and pollution classes were broken into terciles/quantiles.

Zones were dissolved into a maximum of five features per pollutant, summing population (pop_sum) within each pol_class_max class (FRANCE_no2_2023_chart.gpkg and equivalents) — the basis for the pie charts on the Results page.

Explain what the five dissolved classes represent and any surprising exposure result you found.
